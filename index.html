Questions Atlanta Families Ask

Family law, answered

Can a father get full custody in Georgia?+
Yes. Georgia courts decide custody on the best interest of the child, not the parent's gender. A father can be awarded primary physical or joint custody when he can show that arrangement serves the child. For an unmarried father, establishing legitimation is usually the required first step.
What is legitimation and why does it matter?+
In Georgia, an unmarried father generally has no legal custody or visitation rights until he files for legitimation. It legally establishes the father-child relationship and is the gateway to pursuing custody, visitation and parenting time.
How is child support calculated in Georgia?+
Georgia uses an Income Shares model that considers both parents' gross incomes, the number of children and adjustments such as health insurance and childcare, then applies the state's guidelines to reach a presumptive amount.
