Practice Area

Child Custody

Georgia separates legal custody, the right to make decisions about medical care, education and religion, from physical custody, where your child actually lives. Arrangements range from primary custody to a true joint schedule where time is shared closely between both homes.

Courts decide based on the best interest of the child and favor arrangements that keep both parents present. We are passionate about making sure our clients keep a real, lasting presence in their children's lives.

  • Legal and physical custody, joint and primary
  • Detailed, workable parenting plans
  • Parental alienation and relocation matters
  • Custody modifications as circumstances change

Questions Atlanta Families Ask

Family law, answered

Can a father get full custody in Georgia?+
Yes. Georgia courts decide custody on the best interest of the child, not the parent's gender. A father can be awarded primary physical or joint custody when he can show that arrangement serves the child. For an unmarried father, establishing legitimation is usually the required first step.
What is legitimation and why does it matter?+
In Georgia, an unmarried father generally has no legal custody or visitation rights until he files for legitimation. It legally establishes the father-child relationship and is the gateway to pursuing custody, visitation and parenting time.
How is property divided in a Georgia divorce?+
Georgia follows equitable division, which means marital property is divided fairly but not always equally. The court weighs each spouse's contributions, conduct and financial circumstances to reach a fair result.
How is child support calculated in Georgia?+
Georgia uses an Income Shares model that considers both parents' gross incomes, the number of children and adjustments such as health insurance and childcare, then applies the state's guidelines to reach a presumptive amount.
Can a mother stop a father from seeing his child in Georgia?+
Until an unmarried father legitimates, he has no enforceable custody or visitation rights, so access can lawfully be withheld. Once legitimation and a parenting plan are in place, both parents must follow the court order. This is exactly why acting early matters.
How long does a legitimation case take?+
An uncontested legitimation can often resolve in a couple of months. When it is tied to a contested custody dispute it takes longer. We work to move your case efficiently while protecting your position.
How long does a child custody case take in Georgia?+
It depends on whether the case is contested and on the county's docket. Uncontested matters can resolve in weeks; contested custody can run several months to over a year. We give you a realistic timeline at your consultation.
